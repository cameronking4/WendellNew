Historian of the twentieth-century United States, Canada, and the African Diaspora...

In pursuit of radical humanism

My name is Wendell Nii Laryea Adjetey, and I am a doctoral candidate in the Department of History and the Department of African American Studies at Yale University. I received the M.A. and M.Phil. in May 2015 and will earn the Ph.D. in May 2018. I hold numerous awards, prizes, and distinctions, including Yale’s Felix G. Evangelist, Douglass R. Bomeisler, Edla J. McPherson, and Falk Foundation Fellowships. I am a beneficiary of a Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation Doctoral Scholarship, Canada’s most prestigious doctoral award for humanists and social scientists. I have held a Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council (SSHRC) of Canada Doctoral Fellowship. In 2016-2017, I studied as a Visiting Scholar and Senior Resident Fellow at Massey College, University of Toronto. I earned an undergraduate degree in history and political science in 2008 and an M.A. in 2009 in political science and ethnic, immigration, and pluralism studies from the University of Toronto. Currently, I am a Visiting Scholar and Pre-Doctoral Fellow in the History Section at MIT. I am an honorary New Englander through residence, Canadian via naturalization, and Ghanaian by birth.
